# OTC Metals — Platform Philosophy

This document describes the core philosophy of OTC Metals.

It should be used as the starting point for product, UX, legal, trust, registration, order, auction, monetization, and technical decisions.

Before making major product changes, agents and contributors should read this file together with:

- `docs/product-vision.md`
- `docs/launch-standard.md`

## Why OTC Metals exists

OTC Metals is not being built as a simple classifieds board, a classic marketplace, an exchange, or a broker.

OTC Metals is being built as a single digital ecosystem for the precious-metals market.

The real market already exists. Participants buy and sell precious metals through phone calls, messengers, personal contacts, managers, brokers, and informal networks.

The goal of OTC Metals is to bring this existing market into one professional digital environment.

The platform should make the market:

- easier to access;
- more transparent;
- more structured;
- more trustworthy;
- easier to compare;
- easier to navigate legally;
- easier for buyers and sellers to find each other.

## What the platform should feel like

OTC Metals should feel expensive, serious, professional, and simple to use.

The product should not feel like a raw test, a cheap board, or a chaotic list of ads.

It should feel like a trusted working environment for people and companies who operate with real money, real metal, and real legal obligations.

## Market participants

The ecosystem should eventually include all major precious-metals market participants:

- individuals;
- investors;
- pawnshops / lombards;
- buyers / purchasing points;
- jewelers;
- jewelry companies;
- jewelry factories;
- wholesale companies;
- refineries;
- banks;
- institutional participants;
- other legally relevant market actors.

## The market cycle

OTC Metals should reflect the real movement of metal through the market.

Metal may enter the market from an individual, then move to a buyer or pawnshop, then to processing, refining, manufacturing, wholesale, investment, banking, or another professional channel.

The platform should understand this cycle and help each participant find the correct next counterparty.

The long-term vision is a digital ecosystem where the market flow becomes easier to see and easier to navigate.

## Core product idea

OTC Metals is not just a place where users post offers.

OTC Metals is a market-routing system.

A participant enters the platform. The platform should understand:

- who the participant is;
- whether the participant is an individual, sole proprietor, or legal entity;
- what role the participant has in the market;
- what licenses, permissions, documents, and statuses are relevant;
- what taxation or legal restrictions may apply;
- what the participant is allowed to buy;
- what the participant is allowed to sell;
- which counterparties are appropriate;
- which actions should be available;
- which actions should be blocked.

After that, the platform should route the participant only to the opportunities that are suitable for that participant.

## The routing principle

The platform should not connect participants randomly.

It should not show every opportunity to every participant.

It should not allow a user to create or respond to an order that does not match their legal or operational status.

Instead, the platform should act as an intelligent filter:

- this participant can buy this type of metal;
- this participant cannot buy this type of metal;
- this participant can sell this category;
- this participant needs extra verification before this action;
- this participant may work with this counterparty;
- this participant should not be matched with this counterparty.

The goal is to reduce wrong matches before they happen.

## What users should experience

Users should not be forced to understand all legal and documentary complexity.

They should not have to manually compare dozens of documents before even understanding whether the counterparty is relevant.

The platform should do the complex work under the hood.

The user-facing result should be simple:

- verified participant;
- documents checked;
- authority confirmed;
- can buy this category;
- can sell this category;
- allowed scenario;
- not allowed scenario.

The system should explain restrictions clearly when needed, but should not overwhelm users with legal details.

## Trust philosophy

Trust is one of the main products of OTC Metals.

Trust does not mean that OTC Metals guarantees a transaction.

Trust means that the platform has checked the participant as far as reasonably possible through official or reliable sources, and has determined what actions are appropriate for that participant.

The platform should avoid admitting obviously inappropriate, fake, blocked, or high-risk participants.

If a participant is not suitable for the platform, the platform should not let that participant operate as a trusted market actor.

## Documents and storage

OTC Metals should not become a document archive.

The platform should avoid storing unnecessary sensitive documents.

The platform should not store documents just for the sake of storing them.

The goal is to verify and record the result of verification, not to accumulate passports, licenses, contracts, and private transaction documents.

Where possible, the platform should store:

- verification status;
- source of verification;
- date of verification;
- validity period;
- result of the check;
- participant trust level;
- permitted actions.

The platform should avoid publicly showing unnecessary sensitive details.

## What OTC Metals does not do

OTC Metals does not:

- buy metal;
- sell metal;
- store metal;
- store money;
- perform settlements;
- act as broker;
- act as escrow;
- act as guarantor;
- become a party to the transaction;
- guarantee the quality of metal;
- guarantee payment;
- guarantee that a transaction will close.

The transaction itself happens outside the platform between the parties.

## What OTC Metals does

OTC Metals:

- brings market participants into one ecosystem;
- helps buyers find sellers;
- helps sellers find buyers;
- checks participants;
- classifies participants;
- routes participants according to their status;
- shows relevant opportunities;
- hides irrelevant or prohibited scenarios;
- supports negotiation flow;
- helps parties decide whether to reveal contacts;
- records trust signals and platform activity.

## Price and market transparency

A major market problem is price opacity.

Many participants already know each other, but prices are often negotiated through pressure, personal contacts, and fragmented information.

Sellers want a higher price.

Buyers want a lower price.

OTC Metals should make the market easier to compare:

- who is buying;
- who is selling;
- what volume is available;
- what price ranges are visible;
- what discounts or premiums exist relative to market benchmarks;
- what region and participant type are involved.

The goal is not to force a single price, but to make the market more visible and easier to navigate.

## User journey principle

The user journey should lead to a result.

A buyer should be able to enter, understand the market, find relevant sellers, and move toward a purchase.

A seller should be able to enter, understand demand, find relevant buyers, and move toward a sale.

A professional participant should be able to create orders, auctions, or requests without unnecessary friction.

The platform should reduce confusion and help users progress step by step.

## First launch quality

OTC Metals should not be launched as a raw MVP.

The goal is a polished first public version.

The product can be developed in stages internally, but the public user experience should look complete, serious, and trustworthy.

## Strategic direction

The strategic direction is to build the central professional ecosystem of the precious-metals market.

The platform should become the place where market participants go to:

- see the market;
- compare opportunities;
- check counterparties;
- place orders;
- create auctions;
- negotiate;
- find the correct legal and commercial route for their metal.

All future product decisions should be checked against this question:

> Does this help OTC Metals become the trusted routing ecosystem for the precious-metals market?

If yes, the idea may be developed.

If no, the idea should be questioned or rejected.
